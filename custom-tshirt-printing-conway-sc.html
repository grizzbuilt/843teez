How to Find the Right Custom T-Shirt Printer in Conway, SC (Without the Facebook Comment Chaos)

Posted: March 13, 2026 | By: 843 Teez Team

Asking “Who can make 5 shirts ASAP?” in a Facebook group might get you 50 comments—but not always the right answer.

If you’ve ever posted online asking for custom shirts in Conway SC, you already know what happens next.

You ask a simple question like, “Does anyone know someone who can make 5 shirts ASAP?” and suddenly the comments fill up with cousins, friends, hobby printers, random pages, and people tagging people they’ve never even ordered from themselves.

It turns into chaos fast.

The truth is, finding the right person for custom t-shirt printing in Conway SC is not just about who gets recommended the most in a thread. It’s about finding someone who can actually deliver quality, communicate clearly, and handle your order the right way.

Whether you need shirts for a business, family vacation, birthday, event, fundraiser, or just a few last-minute tees, here’s how to choose the right printer without getting lost in the comment pile.

1. Look for Real Examples of Finished Shirts

The first thing to check is simple: can you see real work?

Not just digital mockups. Not just a logo pasted on a blank shirt template. Real printed shirts.

A reliable custom printer should be able to show:

  • photos of actual finished shirts
  • close-up shots of print quality
  • examples of different design styles
  • work done for real customers or local businesses

Anyone can say they make shirts. What matters is whether the finished product actually looks clean, pressed right, and ready to wear.

2. Ask How the Shirts Are Printed

Not all shirt printing is the same, and that matters more than most people realize.

Different print methods work better for different jobs:

DTF (Direct to Film)

Great for full-color artwork, detailed prints, and smaller custom shirt orders.

Screen Printing

Usually best for larger orders with simpler designs and fewer colors.

Heat Transfer Vinyl

Often used for names, numbers, and straightforward one-color personalization.

If someone can’t explain how your shirts will be made, that’s a red flag. A good printer should be able to tell you what method they use and why it makes sense for your specific order.

3. Make Sure They Actually Handle Small Orders

This is a big one.

A lot of shops focus mainly on bulk runs. That’s great if you need 100 shirts. But plenty of people only need 5, 8, or 12.

Small orders are common for:

  • birthday shirts
  • family trip shirts
  • small business staff tees
  • band merch
  • reunion shirts
  • memorial or event shirts

If you only need a few shirts, don’t assume every printer wants that kind of order. Ask directly. Some shops make small-run work easy, and others really only want volume.

4. Ask for a Clear Turnaround Time

“ASAP” means different things to different people.

For one shop, it means same week. For another, it means whenever they get around to it.

Before you commit, ask:

  • How long will this order take?
  • Do you offer rush orders?
  • Do you have local pickup?
  • Will you let me know if there are delays?

Clear communication matters almost as much as print quality. A printer who responds clearly and sets realistic expectations is usually much easier to work with than someone who just says, “Yeah, I got you.”

5. Local Matters More Than You Think

Working with a local printer can make the whole process easier.

If you’re looking for custom shirts in Conway SC or nearby, local usually means:

  • faster communication
  • easier pickup
  • better turnaround for urgent jobs
  • more direct help with design questions
  • supporting a local small business

That’s especially helpful when your order is small, custom, or time-sensitive.

Quick Checklist: Before Ordering Custom Shirts

Before choosing a printer, run through this quick checklist:

  • Can you see real photos of shirts they’ve printed?
  • Do they explain how the shirts will be printed?
  • Do they handle small orders?
  • Do they give a clear turnaround time?
  • Do they communicate clearly and professionally?

If the answer is yes across the board, you’re probably talking to someone solid.

How Much Do Custom Shirts Cost in Conway, SC?

One of the most common questions people ask is: How much do custom shirts cost?

The answer depends on a few things:

Quantity

More shirts usually lowers the price per shirt, but modern methods like DTF make small orders much more doable than they used to be.

Design Complexity

Full-color artwork, detailed prints, and oversized designs can affect pricing depending on the method and garment.

Shirt Choice

Basic cotton tees, soft ringspun shirts, premium garments, and performance tees all come at different price points.

Artwork Prep

If your artwork is ready to print, that saves time. If you have an idea but need help bringing it to life, there may be some design setup involved.

The best way to get a real answer is to reach out with your quantity, timeline, and design idea so a printer can quote the actual job instead of guessing.
