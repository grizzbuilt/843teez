How Much Do Custom Shirts Cost in Conway, SC?

Posted: April 10, 2026 | By: 843 Teez Team

The real answer is not “it depends” and then silence. It does depend — but it depends on a few specific things, and once you know them, custom shirt pricing makes a whole lot more sense.

Custom Shirt Pricing in Conway, SC

If you’re looking for custom shirts in Conway, SC, one of the first questions you probably have is how much it’s all going to cost. That’s fair. Nobody wants to waste time sending messages back and forth just to find out the price is nowhere near the budget.

The good news is that custom shirt pricing is pretty easy to understand once you know what drives it. Most small-run custom shirts start around $20 each, but the real number depends on your quantity, artwork, garment choice, and the print method that makes the most sense for your order.

Around here, we’d rather explain it clearly than hide behind vague pricing. So here’s the straight answer.

What Actually Affects the Cost of Custom Shirts?

1. Quantity Changes Everything

The number of shirts you need is usually the biggest pricing factor.

If you only need one shirt, five shirts, or ten shirts, the price per piece will be higher than a larger run. That’s normal. With a smaller order, there are fewer pieces to spread the work across.

On the other hand, when you order in bigger quantities, the price per shirt usually drops. That’s why a 50-shirt order often has a very different per-shirt price than a 5-shirt order, even if the design is the same.

2. The Print Method Matters

Not all custom shirts are printed the same way, and that matters when it comes to pricing.

For smaller orders and full-color designs, DTF printing in Conway, SC is usually the best fit. It works great for one-offs, short runs, detailed artwork, and designs with multiple colors.

For larger runs, screen printing often gives you better value, especially if the design is simple and uses one or two colors.

That means the “cheapest” method isn’t always the same across every order. The right method depends on what you actually need.

3. Design Complexity Changes the Price

A small left-chest logo is different from a full front print. A simple one-color design is different from a detailed full-color graphic. A single print location is different from front-and-back printing.

The more complicated the artwork, the more time, materials, and setup usually go into it. That doesn’t mean complex designs are a bad idea. It just means they may cost more than something simpler.

If you already have artwork ready to go, that helps. If you only have an idea, a screenshot, or a rough concept, that’s still workable — it just may need a little more prep.

4. Shirt Brand and Garment Type

Not all blank shirts cost the same.

A basic cotton tee will usually be less expensive than a soft ringspun shirt, heavyweight garment, premium brand tee, long sleeve, hoodie, or performance shirt. Sizes matter too. Extended sizes can cost more than standard sizes depending on the garment.

If price is the main concern, we can usually recommend shirt options that still look good without blowing up the budget.

What’s a Real-World Price Range?

There’s no single number that fits every custom shirt job, but here’s a realistic way to think about it:

  • 1 custom shirt: usually starts around $20
  • Small runs like 5–12 shirts: often land in the mid-teens to around $20 each, depending on artwork and garment
  • Larger runs: price per shirt usually drops as quantity goes up

That’s why asking “How much is a shirt?” without quantity, design, or garment info usually only gets you a rough ballpark. The more details you have, the more accurate the quote gets.

What’s the Cheapest Way to Order Custom Shirts?

If your goal is to keep costs down, a few things help:

  • Order more shirts if you can
  • Keep the artwork simple
  • Stick to one print location when possible
  • Use a standard shirt style instead of a premium garment
  • Give yourself enough lead time and avoid rush pressure

That said, cheapest is not always best. Sometimes spending a little more gets you a better shirt, a cleaner print, or a result you’ll actually be proud to wear.

Are Custom Shirts Worth It for Small Orders?

Absolutely — especially now.

Years ago, custom printing made more sense mainly for bulk runs. But now, methods like DTF make it a lot easier to do smaller orders without massive setup costs. That’s good news if you need birthday shirts, family trip shirts, test runs for a brand, staff shirts for a small team, or just a few custom pieces for something specific.

How to Get an Accurate Quote Fast

The fastest way to get a real number is to send:

  • How many shirts you need
  • Sizes
  • Shirt color
  • Print location (front, back, left chest, sleeve, etc.)
  • Your artwork or idea
  • Your deadline

Once you’ve got that, the guesswork mostly disappears.

The Bottom Line

Custom shirt prices in Conway, SC are not random. They’re based on a few real factors: quantity, print method, artwork, and garment choice.

For a lot of small orders, shirts start around $20. Larger runs can bring the per-shirt price down. The best quote comes from matching the right print method to the job instead of forcing every order into the same setup.

In other words: the right custom shirt order doesn’t start with a made-up price — it starts with the right questions.
