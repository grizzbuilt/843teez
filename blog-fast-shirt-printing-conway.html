How Fast Can You Actually Get Shirts?

The honest answer: it depends—but not in a vague way.

Turnaround time usually comes down to three things:

  • How many shirts you need
  • What printing method is used
  • How ready your design is

Small orders with ready-to-print artwork can often be done in just a few days. Larger or more complex orders take longer.

What Slows Orders Down

  • No artwork or unclear design
  • Special-order shirt colors or sizes
  • Large bulk orders
  • Slow approval during proofing
  • Last-minute changes

Most delays don’t come from printing—they come from missing info or waiting on decisions.

What About Shirt Availability?

One thing most people don’t realize: the shirts themselves don’t always live in the shop.

Most print shops, including us, source blanks from suppliers, which usually takes a couple of days depending on the style, size range, and color you choose.

That doesn’t mean your order is slow—it just means part of the timeline is getting the right shirts in. Because we’re local, once the shirts arrive, production can move fast.

  • Common colors and sizes are typically faster to get
  • Specialty colors or extended sizes may take longer
  • Flexibility can speed things up significantly

If speed matters most, we’ll guide you toward options that are available quickly so your order stays on track.

How to Get Your Shirts Faster

  • Have your design ready or a clear idea
  • Know your sizes and quantities
  • Choose in-stock or easy-to-source shirt colors
  • Respond quickly when we send proofs
  • Be flexible if needed

Local vs Online (Big Difference)
