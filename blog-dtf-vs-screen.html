The biggest mistake people make? Choosing the wrong printing method for their order.

The Short Answer

If you're ordering small quantities (1–24 shirts), DTF is usually the best choice.

If you're ordering 25+ shirts with simple designs, screen printing usually wins.

If you're also trying to understand pricing behind those choices, check out our custom shirt pricing guide .

DTF vs Screen Printing (Quick Comparison)

DTF Printing

  • No minimums
  • Full color designs
  • Fast turnaround
  • Best for small orders

Screen Printing

  • Best for bulk orders
  • Simple designs
  • Lower cost per shirt at volume
  • Requires setup time

1. Design Complexity

If your design includes gradients, multiple colors, or detailed artwork—DTF wins every time.

Screen printing is better for clean, simple designs like logos or text with one or two colors.

2. Quantity Matters Most

This is where most decisions are made:

  • 1–24 shirts → DTF
  • 25+ shirts → Screen Printing

Trying to force screen printing on a small order usually ends up costing more, not less.

If you want a step-by-step way to decide, check out our guide to choosing the best printing method .

3. Turnaround Time

DTF is faster because there’s no setup required.

Screen printing takes longer upfront because screens have to be created before printing begins.

4. Cost Breakdown

DTF keeps costs consistent for small orders.

Screen printing becomes cheaper per shirt as quantity increases.

What Most People Get Wrong

A lot of people assume screen printing is always cheaper.

That’s only true for bulk orders.

For small runs with full-color designs, DTF is almost always the better option.
