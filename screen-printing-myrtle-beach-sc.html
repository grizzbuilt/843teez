What Is Screen Printing?

Screen printing applies ink directly to fabric through a stencil, or “screen.” It’s the classic method used for decades because it creates clean, durable prints and becomes very cost-effective when you’re ordering in volume.

When Screen Printing Is the Best Choice

Bulk Orders

Once screens are set up, the cost per shirt drops fast, which makes screen printing a strong fit for 25 or more shirts.

Simple, Bold Artwork

Best for 1–2 solid colors like company logos, team names, school shirts, and classic merch designs.

Durability

Tough, classic prints that hold up well for work shirts, event tees, uniforms, and high-wear use.

Screen Printing vs. DTF

Choose Screen Printing if…

  • You need 25+ shirts
  • Your artwork is 1–2 solid colors
  • You want the best bulk value
  • You need shirts for staff, teams, schools, or events

Choose DTF if…

  • You need 1–24 shirts
  • Your design is full color or detailed
  • You want no minimums
  • You need a better option for gradients, photos, or complex artwork

Screen Printing Pricing: What Affects Cost?

Screen printing usually has a setup cost for screen creation and then a lower per-shirt rate as quantity increases. The main pricing factors are:

  • Quantity and sizes
  • Number of ink colors, especially if the design goes beyond 1–2 colors
  • Print locations like front, back, left chest, and sleeve
  • Garment type, such as tees, hoodies, tanks, or performance shirts
  • Deadline or event date
