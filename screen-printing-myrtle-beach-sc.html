What Is Screen Printing?

Screen printing applies ink directly to fabric through a stencil (“screen”). It’s the classic method used for decades because it’s durable, looks great, and becomes very cost-effective when you’re ordering in volume.

When Screen Printing Is the Best Choice

Bulk Orders (25+)

Once screens are set up, the cost per shirt drops fast—perfect for larger groups.

Simple, Bold Artwork

Best for 1–2 solid colors like logos, team names, and clean designs.

Max Durability

Classic, tough prints that hold up great for work shirts and high-wear use.

Screen Printing vs. DTF

Choose Screen Printing if…

  • You need 25+ shirts
  • Your art is 1–2 solid colors
  • You want the best bulk price

Choose DTF if…

  • You need 1–24 shirts
  • Your design is full color or detailed
  • You want no minimums

Screen Printing Pricing (What Affects Cost)

Screen printing usually has a setup cost (screen creation) and then a lower per-shirt rate as quantity increases. The main pricing factors are:

  • Quantity + sizes
  • Number of ink colors (best for 1–2 colors)
  • Print locations (front/back/sleeve)
  • Garment type (tees vs hoodies vs performance shirts)
  • Deadline / event date

Screen Printing FAQ

What’s the best minimum for screen printing?

Most orders make sense at 25+ units. Under that, DTF is usually a better value.

How many colors can you do?

Screen printing is best for 1–2 solid colors. If your design is full color, DTF is ideal.
